Training

The Special Education Support Center offers training to schools, districts, community groups, advocacy organizations, and individuals upon request.  We offer high quality training with effective/responsive follow-up coaching from a catalog of standard workshops as well as customized workshops tailored to the unique needs/circumstances of the schools/district we serve.

The standard trainings/workshops offered by the Special Education Support Center are six hours each, with the exception of Brain Research and Effective Instruction, which is a total of 12 hours. Each training can be scheduled to meet the needs of individual groups.  The current catalog includes:

Autism Awareness

Clarifies the role of general and special educators in developing appropriate goals and programs for students with Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD). Discussions include intervention strategies, access to general education, and supports for improved organization, communication, social and behavioral skills.

Behavior Interventions

Designed to provide participants with the opportunity to explore their attitudes and understanding  student behaviors and the use of positive behavior interventions to change those behaviors.

Brain Research & Effective Instruction

Learn the basic physiology of the brain and the fundamentals of how the brain organizes, recalls and uses information- how learning occurs. The workshop enables educators to apply effective instructional strategies addressing both procedural and declarative knowledge in the development of student programs.  Activities include hands-on use of information on effective instructional strategies and connecting the use of those strategies to current brain research.

Standards-Based Special Ed

Develop an understanding of the organizational structure and content of the Common Core Standards and learn a method for unpacking the standards in order to write IEP goals aligned with the standards, while ensuring that students receive appropriate specially designed instruction.  Incorporates an exploration of Universal Design for Learning as a means of providing support for their students in their general and special education classes.

IEP non-transition

Presents the Rules & Regulations (WAC) that guide the development of the IEP, and focuses on the intent of the IEP process to develop meaningful and individualized IEPs through a collaborative process. Topics include: developing an IEP from the evaluation; assessing present level of academic, functional and behavioral performance; development of appropriate goals; specially designed instruction; and progress monitoring.

IEP with transition

Presents the Rules and Regulations (WAC) which guide the development of the IEP, and focuses on the intent of the IEP process to develop meaningful and individualized IEPs through a collaborative process. Topics include: understanding the elements of an appropriate assessment and planning for transition; assessing the present level of academic, functional and behavioral performance; development of appropriate transition and IEP goals; specially designed instruction; and progress monitoring.

Section 504

Participants will gain understanding of: the definition of FAPE under Section 504, procedural requirements for evaluation of and programming for students with disabilities, and the eligibility requirements for Section 504 protections.

Special Ed Law

Creates a framework used to create programs for students with disabilities. It outlines the responsibilities for educators and the rights for students with disabilities. Violations of the laws can cause school districts to lose money or be required to do additional work through complaint and/or monitoring processes. Participants in this workshop develop understand of how laws, rules, and regulations affect practice in providing programs for students in special education.  Discussions include:
  • Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE),
  • Appropriate student evaluation,
  • Individualized Education Program (IEP),
  • Least Restrictive Environment (LRE),
  • Specially Designed Instruction (SDI),
  • Parent and student participation in decision-making,
  • Procedural due process, and Discipline.

Universal Design for Learning

Participants develop understanding of how universal design improves learning for all students: recognizing the elements of UDL and its principles, utilizing examples of universal design, creating classroom lessons using UDL principles, and incorporating Universal Design into instructional plans.
